Before we talk about recruitment, let's just clear up one thing. Nobody wants to work for you just for the privilege of working for you. Like every rule, there are, of course, exceptions. There are some organisations that are so prominent that some people desperately want to work for them (we'll talk about this a little more later). And there are some people who are so motivated by a particular organisation's mission or values that they feel strongly motivated to be a part of that.

But the likelihood that this is your scenario is infinitesimal. So, just to be clear and to double down on the point, let's say it again: Nobody want's to work for you just for the privilege of working for you.

First and foremost, people get up and go to work every day for the money. It is obviously true that there are other factors that motivate people - some people relish a particular challenge, or derive job satisfaction from the work they do. Others like the social aspect or camaraderie of their team. There are other things too. But these are all secondary. If people didn't need the money, they wouldn't come and work for you.

That may seem cynical, and there are certainly exceptions. But it's important to frame the rest of the discussion in these terms.
